India Evidence Notes
GP·TW3·BoneXpert·digiBONE — evidence for Indian children

🇮🇳 Indian Evidence in One Paragraph

In a large Pune cohort (851 children), TW3-RUS was closest to expert consensus overall — especially for girls and prepubertal boys — while GP and GR showed age/sex bias. Later studies (2024) validated BoneXpert v3 in 920 healthy Indian children; error was larger in boys and GP50 best matched manual rating. The Indian 3-bone method (radius + hamate + DP3) had RMSE ~0.6–0.7 y vs GP/TW in 493 films. digiBONE (2026, Indian-domain CNNs) achieved MAD 4.75 months in boys, 4.93 months in girls. Indian studies consistently find relatively delayed carpal maturity, especially in boys — segment-aware rating is recommended. No nationally representative 0–18 Indian atlas yet exists.

⚠️ Three Core Cautions

  • Do not use CA as ground truth. A healthy child can normally mature earlier or later. Validation should use expert consensus, not correlation alone.
  • Carpal–RUS discordance matters. Carpals ossify by appositional growth; tubular bones mature through physes and are more sex-steroid responsive. Report major dissociation rather than averaging.
  • Not for legal age declaration. A hand film alone cannot establish exact chronological age or an 18-year threshold. Forensic protocols and jurisdiction-specific standards are separate.

🔬 Clinical Workflow (6 Steps)

  1. Confirm indication and identity — record sex, exact CA, height/velocity, pubertal stage, diagnosis.
  2. Acquire standard PA left hand/wrist — palm flat, fingers separated, middle finger aligned with forearm.
  3. Quality and anatomy screen — check rotation, flexion, motion, exposure, fracture, prior surgery, dysplasia.
  4. Read three segments — distal radius/ulna → carpals → metacarpals/phalanges. Record segmental disagreement.
  5. Apply one declared method — GP, TW3-RUS, BoneXpert, digiBONE, or Indian 3-bone. State version and reference population.
  6. Integrate, do not over-claim — interpret BA–CA with growth, puberty, nutrition and disease context.
